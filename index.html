SWOT Analysis
Strategic evaluation based on menu sales data · Beef burritos, Chicken, Breakfast, and underperforming SKUs
S
Strengths
What's working well
  • Beef burritos drive 27.4% of total revenue from just 3 SKUs — the most efficient category
  • Top 5 items generate $17,081 — 49.3% of all revenue from only 8% of the SKU count
  • Chicken Flautas is a standout #2 category (14.5% share) with only 2 SKUs
  • Chicken bites & wings command the highest avg price at $5.22 — premium positioning is working
  • Strong volume: Beef Bean Red Chili Burrito alone moved 2,290 units — proven demand
W
Weaknesses
Areas needing attention
  • Burgers: 9 SKUs generating only $1,207 (3.5% share) — worst revenue-per-SKU in the menu
  • 25 of 63 SKUs (40%) generate under $100 each — dead inventory creating operational drag
  • Chicken sandwiches: 4 SKUs totaling only $371 — barely justifying their menu slot
  • Single-item concentration risk: #1 item (Beef Bean Burrito) = 19.1% of ALL revenue
  • Breakfast burritos fragmented across 4 SKUs with combined $646 — low focus and output
O
Opportunities
Growth levers to pull
  • Chicken wings 10pc sells at $11.81 avg — customers will pay premium; expand this tier
  • Specialty burritos: only 1 SKU, already $584 revenue — strong signal to expand the line
  • Breakfast croissants outperform biscuits despite same SKU count (5 each) — shift promotion focus
  • Sausage on a stick ranks 4th in units (578) but only 5th in revenue — promotion could unlock more
  • Fried cheese snacks: 3 SKUs, solid $1,163 revenue — room to add one premium SKU
T
Threats
Risks to address
  • Menu bloat: 63 SKUs adds prep complexity and waste — bottom 25 SKUs generating under $100 each
  • Beef burrito supply dependency: one SKU disruption = 19% revenue at risk
  • Burger category competing against QSR players with better products and brand — losing the battle
  • Chicken sandwich segment (4 SKUs, $371 total) likely cannibalized by stronger chicken categories
  • Bottom 12 SKUs under $100 revenue may be creating customer confusion and inventory waste
